I navigated to the cs.CL (Computation and Language) recent submissions listing, selected the most recent paper (entry #1, submitted 24 Jun 2026), and opened its abstract page.

The selected paper is **"Real-Time Voice AI Hears but Does Not Listen"** (arXiv:2606.26083) by Martijn Bartelds, Federico Bianchi, and James Zou.

**Abstract:** Speech conveys information through both words and vocal delivery. The authors evaluate four leading production real-time voice systems — OpenAI's GPT Realtime 2, Google's Gemini 3.1 Flash Live, and Alibaba's Qwen3.5 Omni Plus and Omni Flash — on tasks where both the words and the delivery patterns convey meaningful information. Across three consequential scenarios, all four systems act on the words rather than the voice: they end calls with crying callers who insist nothing is wrong, approve wire transfers authorized in frightened voices, and enroll callers whose agreement is clearly sarcastic. Surprisingly, this is often not a failure of perception — three of the four systems reliably identify the distress, fear, or sarcasm they later ignore when making decisions. The authors term this disconnect the "emotional intelligence gap" of voice AI, and find that prompting systems to attend to vocal delivery improves performance only partially, cautioning against use in settings where tone and emotion convey important information.
