**Article:** *"Probing Kinematic Anisotropies in the Stochastic Gravitational Wave Background with the SKA"* (arXiv:2606.25845), by N. M. J. Cruz, A. Malhotra, G. Tasinato, and I. Zavala — submitted 24 June 2026.

**Main findings:** The authors forecast how well the Square Kilometre Array Observatory (SKAO) can probe anisotropies in the nanohertz stochastic gravitational-wave background (SGWB) using Pulsar Timing Arrays. Their key target is the *kinematic dipole* induced by the Solar System's motion, whose detection would confirm the SGWB's cosmological origin. Using optimal estimators and Fisher techniques across the baseline AA4 configuration, an optimistic 1000-pulsar scenario, and a joint analysis with Gaia-like astrometry, they conclude that while SKAO will substantially tighten constraints on SGWB anisotropies, even combined analyses fall short of the sensitivity needed to detect the kinematic dipole — motivating new observational and analysis strategies.
