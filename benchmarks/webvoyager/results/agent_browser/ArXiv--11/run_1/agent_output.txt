Based on arXiv's FAQ page **"Submissions in Languages Other Than English and Multiple-Language Submissions"** (https://arxiv.org/faq/multilang.html):

- **Do you need a multi-language abstract?** Yes. arXiv accepts non-English submissions but requires that every submission include an **English version of the abstract**, prepared so the English and alternate-language abstracts follow one another (kept as plain ASCII text, no TeX/HTML formatting).
- **Separator between the multiple abstracts:** a line of **five dashes `-----`**, with a **blank line before and after** it.

Example as documented:
```
English version of abstract for your important paper.

-----

Other language version of abstract for your important paper.
```

Sources: [Non-English submissions FAQ (current)](https://info.arxiv.org/help/faq/multilang.html), [Multilingual abstract guidance](https://arxiv.org/faq/multilang.html)

ANSWER: Yes — for non-English submissions you must include an English version of the abstract alongside the other-language abstract. The separator between the multiple abstracts is a line of five dashes "-----" (with a blank line before and after it).
