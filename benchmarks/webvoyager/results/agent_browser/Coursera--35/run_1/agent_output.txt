**Course: Sustainable Agricultural Land Management** — offered by the University of Florida on Coursera (Beginner level, 8 modules, ~2 weeks, rated 4.8/5 from 669 reviews; 58,470 enrolled).

**Course objectives:** The course covers agricultural and urban water-quality issues in Florida — their causes, land and nutrient management strategies, and the science and policy behind Best Management Practices (BMPs). Students learn to evaluate BMP research and analyze its role in shaping practices and policies that protect water quality. The 8 modules are: (1) Introduction to Sustainable Agricultural Land Management, (2) Water Quality Issues and Regulations, (3) TMDLs & Nutrients, (4) Best Management Practices, (5) Soil Management, (6) Nutrient Management, (7) Water for Agriculture, and (8) Agricultural Irrigation Management.

**Lead instructor — George J. Hochmuth, Ph.D.:** A Professor at the University of Florida (243 instructor ratings). He leads a Research Program in Urban and Agricultural Fertilization and Nutrient Management Practices. His research addresses environmental-science questions on water quality and nutrient/water management in urban and agricultural settings — focusing on nitrogen and phosphorus, irrigation and fertilization efficiency, keeping nutrients in the plant root zone, developing soil/plant testing tools, and systems approaches (crop selection, rotation, cover-cropping, mulching) to minimize nutrient losses. His work spans greenhouse bench-scale studies, field-scale studies on farms and residential landscapes, and hydroponics.
