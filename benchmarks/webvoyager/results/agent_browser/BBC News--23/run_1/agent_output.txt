**Latest health news:** *"Want to feel happier at work? Take a five-minute walk"* (BBC News Health, by Digital health editor Michelle Roberts, posted 2 days ago).

**Main findings & recommendations:**
- A new study in the *British Journal of Sports Medicine* (Columbia University, surveying 11,000+ mostly office-based US employees) tested different "movement snack" schedules during 8–9 hour shifts.
- **Key recommendation:** Take a **five-minute walking break every hour**. This delivered the biggest and most realistic improvement in **productivity, mood and alertness** without hurting work efficiency.
- Walking every half hour also helped mood/fatigue but was too disruptive to work; every two hours was better than nothing but less effective.
- **Why it matters:** Most adults now spend ~three-quarters of their waking day sedentary, and prolonged sitting raises the risk of being overweight and developing heart disease and type 2 diabetes.
- Lead researcher Keith Diaz notes movement breaks can boost executive function, attention and memory, and suggests low-cost tactics like walking meetings or pacing during phone calls.
- **Caveat:** A British Heart Foundation cardiac nurse welcomed the findings but cautioned the study used self-reported, short-term data, so longer research is needed to confirm heart-health benefits.
