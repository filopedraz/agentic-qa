**Latest paper:** *"Emergence of Gamma-Type Upward-Phase Statistics in the Collatz Map: An Effective Poisson Process Mechanism"* (arXiv:2606.26811), by Weicheng Fu, Xiaobin Liu, and Yisen Wang.

**Submission date:** Submitted 25 Jun 2026 (v1: Thu, 25 Jun 2026 09:51:49 UTC); it heads the most recent listing dated Fri, 26 Jun 2026.

**Abstract summary:** The paper explains a statistical regularity in the Collatz map. When each orbit is split into upward and downward steps, the number of upward phases (N↑) empirically follows an approximate Gamma distribution. The authors give a mechanistic account by modeling upward phases in the odd-compressed (Syracuse) version of the map as a homogeneous Poisson process. Using a mean-field logarithmic balance and the geometric distribution of 2-adic valuations, they derive closed-form Gamma parameters: a constant scale parameter θ = 2/(2−log₂3)² ≈ 11.61, and a shape parameter K that grows logarithmically with the maximal initial value X₀ = 2L+1. They also analyze periodic-orbit closure conditions, finding nontrivial cycles severely constrained, which supports the framework. Numerical tests for L from 10⁵ to 10¹⁵ confirm the theory with relative errors below 3% (down to 10⁻³–10⁻²% with bias correction). The work links the arithmetic of the Collatz map to Gamma-type statistics and suggests extensions to generalized Collatz-type problems. (Comments: 7 pages, 5 figures.)
