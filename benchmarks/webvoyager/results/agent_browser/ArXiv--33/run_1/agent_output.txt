"Jet impingement cooling with multi-stage ducted electroaerodynamic actuators" (arXiv:2606.27338)

**Authors:** Quinna Nguyen, C. Luke Nelson, Daniel S. Drew

**Main objective/hypothesis:** The paper hypothesizes that compact arrays of reduced-scale electroaerodynamic (EAD) plasma actuators — silent, solid-state devices with no moving parts — can replace conventional fans for direct jet impingement cooling of mobile/high-performance electronics. Its main contribution is the first rigorous experimental demonstration and system-level validation of multi-stage, ducted EAD jet arrays as a compact fan-replacement cooling solution. The authors characterize single- and multi-stage actuators (heat transfer coefficients, cooling profiles), quantify how added ion-acceleration stages raise jet velocity and heat transfer (at reduced efficiency), benchmark an array against a comparable fan, and integrate it onto a commercial edge AI system — showing thermal regulation during inference workloads matches a stock fan at a fraction of the volume, weight, power, and noise.
