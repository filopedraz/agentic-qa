I located the BBC article "What is climate change? A really simple guide" and read its "How are humans causing climate change?" section.

According to the article, the rapid warming over the last century has been **without doubt caused by human activities** — in particular:

- **The widespread burning of fossil fuels — coal, oil and gas** — in homes, factories and transport systems.
- When these fossil fuels burn, they release **greenhouse gases, mostly carbon dioxide (CO2)**, which acts like a blanket trapping extra energy in the atmosphere near the Earth's surface, causing the planet to heat up.
- Since the Industrial Revolution, this has raised atmospheric CO2 by more than 50%, far above recent historical levels, and the CO2's distinctive chemical fingerprint matches what's being found in the atmosphere.

ANSWER: Human activities are causing climate change — chiefly the widespread burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil and gas) in homes, factories and transport, which releases greenhouse gases (mostly carbon dioxide). This CO2 acts like a blanket trapping heat in the atmosphere, warming the planet; since the Industrial Revolution it has raised atmospheric CO2 by more than 50%.
