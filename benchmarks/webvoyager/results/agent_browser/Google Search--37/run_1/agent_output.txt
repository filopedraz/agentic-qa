**Top 3 super-Earth planets (current, most notable):**

1. **LHS 1140 b** — Widely described as one of the *best candidates in the search for signs of life*. It's a transiting rocky super-Earth (~5–6 Earth masses, ~1.7 Earth radii) sitting in the habitable zone of LHS 1140, a quiet red dwarf about 49 light-years away. Recent observations hint it could be a water/ocean world with a temperate climate.

2. **55 Cancri e** — The archetypal super-Earth and the first whose atmosphere was ever detected. About 8 Earth masses, it orbits a Sun-like star ~41 light-years away on a blistering ~18-hour orbit, making it an ultra-hot "lava world" with a likely molten surface — not habitable, but the most intensely studied super-Earth.

3. **Kepler-442b** — One of the most Earth-like confirmed exoplanets, with a very high Earth Similarity Index. This super-Earth (~2.3 Earth masses) orbits in the habitable zone of a cooler K-type star roughly 1,200 light-years away, where liquid surface water could plausibly exist.
